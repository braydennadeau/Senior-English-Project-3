The Benefits of Video Games

Are Video Games Secretly Making You Smarter?

By Brayden Nadeau

A New Perspective on Gaming

Have you ever heard that video games are nothing but a distraction for students? A great number of people believe that by wasting their time on video games, students lose the opportunity to focus on their studies, rest and deal with everyday things. However, is this an old-fashioned point of view? Is it possible that video games could be helping develop your brain, reaction speed and your everyday abilities?

Over the past few decades, researchers have looked into how video games influence development in the brain and body. Research shows that playing video games helps to improve your hand-eye coordination, memory concentration and decision making, and can help in the development of many more important life skills.

Quick Facts About Video Games

  • The average age of a gamer is 33 years old, making it a lifelong activity.
  • Surgeons who play video games make 37% fewer mistakes and perform 27% faster.
  • Different games build different skills: Action games build focus, while strategy games build planning.
  • Gaming improves brain efficiency in areas responsible for attention and decision making.
  • 64% of people you know likely play video games!

What Age Do People Start Developing Hand-Eye Coordination?

Kids begin to develop hand-eye coordination from a young age. Even before kids are born, or shortly after birth, babies begin to coordinate what they see with how they move. In order to continue growing their coordination and other motor skills, kids will take part in activities like writing, playing sports, walking, running, etc., until they enter elementary school.

At the point of elementary school, although kids' hand-eye coordination would have already developed significantly over the years, it is still developing. This development stage is very important to the overall growth of hand-eye coordination. At this young age, playing video games with your friends may be a big part of a young kid's life. Video game players have to react immediately to visual data presented on the screen with their hands. Therefore, video games serve as one of many forms of practice for developing hand-eye coordination for kids at critical stages of development.

What Age Do People Start Playing Video Games?

Many gamers today begin to play video games when they are still in their childhood or preteen years, typically about 5-10 years old. The trend of playing video games continues into being an adult and is not just exclusively for children.

As stated by Daphne Bavelier in her TED Talk, the average age of a gamer is 33 years old. Video games have become one of those few activities we continue through our lifetime. Unlike many things such as sports where at some point you must stop—no matter if that means graduating and no longer having access to the sport or your body giving out in the pros and having to retire—it will come to an end at some point. Video games are different, and they have a lasting effect on developing minds, which make video game effects long-term.

How Do Different Types Of Games Affect the Brain Differently?

Most video games do not have the same effects as each other when it comes to how they impact your brain. Games are made for many different reasons and to build different skills. Action games will develop a player's ability to focus. Strategy games will help build the player's ability to plan ahead and think critically. Puzzle games will enhance the player's ability to solve problems. Simulations may even be used to better understand the world around us. Because of this, researchers believe that the type of game you play is much more important than how often or how long somebody has played.

How Do Video Games Transfer to Real Life Tasks?

Video game skills aren't limited to just your TV screen; they can have an impact on how you perform in the real world. There is a strong example of this with doctors. HowStuffWorks reported that surgical teams made 37% fewer mistakes and performed operations 27% faster when one surgeon was a video game player compared to the team without a gamer.

The Surgeon Advantage

For example, drivers, athletes, etc. Gamers may be able to react faster during a stressful situation. They will most likely be more attentive and remember things better in school, helping them be more involved and receive higher grades.

What Parts Of The Brain Are Most Stimulated By Video Games?

🧠 The Gamer's Brain

Studies show that repeated gaming actively increases efficiency and neural connections in these specific regions:

Parietal Cortex

Responsible for shifting and maintaining intense visual attention across the screen.

Frontal Lobe

Handles complex problem solving and rapid, real-time decision making.

Anterior Cingulate

Maintains high-level focus and regulates emotion during conflict situations.

Motor Cortex

Builds muscle memory, allowing gamers to react physically without thinking.

What Type Of Studies Have Already Been Done To Test The Impact Of Video Games?

Scientists have done a variety of research on how video games can affect the mind and body. One study in behavioral science shows that highly trained video game players outperformed those with less training when completing reaction and memory tests.

Another study in motor skills demonstrated that video game players were able to complete hand-eye coordination tasks at a higher level than non-video game players.

The NIH completed a very large-scale study that demonstrated that children who took part in playing video games also completed memory and impulse control tasks at a higher level than those who don't play video games.

In conclusion, most of the studies that look at the effects of video games on a person take place by creating a group of people who don't play and a separate group who do play video games, and have them complete tasks that require you to use a skill like hand-eye coordination.

What Tools Measure Reaction Time, Coordination And Cognitive Performance?

There are many tools that scientists use to measure how gaming affects the brain. These include reaction time tests, memory tasks, eye-tracking devices, and brain imaging. Brain imaging is when scientists scan your active brain while doing a task; this shows what parts of your brain that specific task is activating and how much it is engaged in that task.

Why This Matters And The Impact of Gaming

The topic of video games is important because they are an important part of our generation. According to WePlay Holding, a massive portion of the population plays video games. That means on average 64% of the people you know play video games! Instead of seeing them as only a distraction, people should understand how gaming can help develop useful skills. Skills like faster thinking, better memory, and improved focus can help in school, sports, and future careers.

Conclusion

Video games are much more than a form of entertainment. Video games also serve as an educational tool for improving brain function and enhancing your ability to perform better in real-world situations. Studies have proved that playing video games improves hand-eye coordination, helps improve focus and attention, and enhances your memory and decision-making skills.

As with almost anything, excessive video game play can have its negative impacts, but moderate video game play will show positive results. We should no longer be asking the question: "Are video games bad or good?" We should be asking: "What potential benefits can I gain from using video games?"
